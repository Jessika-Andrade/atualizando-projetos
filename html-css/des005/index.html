Some Things I Still Can't Tell You: Poems

Misha Collins

Synopsis

This book is a compilation of small observations and musings. It's filled with moments of reflection and a love letter to simple joys: passing a simple blade of grass on the sidewalk, the freedom of peeing outdoors late at night, or the way a hand-built ceramic mug feels when it's full of warm tea on a chilly morning. It's a catalog and a compendium that examines the complicated experience of being all too human and interacting with a complex, confounding, breathtaking world... and a reminder to stop and be awake and alive in yourself.

These Days (Poem)

I've been doing this funny thing in the afternoons,
This going and lying down
In our bed thing,
Slightly curled on my side
In my jeans and shoes,
Staring at the back side of the blinds,
Crying.

My puffed cheek soaks our pillow
And I cry.
No known reason-
Just tears on my side,
Slightly fetal,
Hidden,
Sad to my bones.

My Hobbies

Instead of seeing them as separate activities, I think of reading, watching movies, and binging TV shows as a single, multi-faceted hobby centered around storytelling. There is a certain magic to getting lost in a good book, where the words on the page conjure up entire worlds, and an equally captivating experience in seeing a story brought to life on the big screen or through a well-crafted television series. One moment, I might be diving into the rich details of a novel, and the next, I'm analyzing the director's choices in its film adaptation, appreciating the unique strengths each medium brings. There’s a distinct, irreplaceable pleasure that comes from the communal experience of going to the cinema, the lights dimming and the shared silence before a story begins. It’s all part of the same passion for narrative, a continuous journey across different formats and worlds.
